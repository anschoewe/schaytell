Welcome

Hello, and welcome! We are thrilled that each of you will join in our marriage merriment this fall! We got engaged last June while enjoying s’mores in New York’s Hudson Valley (hence those gooey Save the Date cards). We are excited to return to the Hudson Valley to get married this October. The weekend will be full of food, fun and fall foliage.

The Hudson Valley is beautiful in the fall, so we encourage you to come and spend the weekend exploring the area. We’ve enjoyed hikes, delicious food, handpicked apples and outdoor art installations. October is also the busiest month in the valley, so we recommend booking travel, accommodations and dining reservations early.

We will be updating this page with additional details as the big event approaches, and formal wedding invitations will be forthcoming. In the meantime, should you have any questions, please feel free to reach out to us. Thanks for visiting!

Toodles,
Devon & Andrew

Accommodations

Wedding accommodations are located in the nearby towns of Cold Spring and Fishkill. We have reserved several room blocks for wedding guests. All rooms include full breakfast. Room blocks expire 4-8 weeks before the wedding, so please book early!

The village of Cold Spring is a small town on the Hudson River, which can be reached by car or train. You can walk to restaurants, antique stores and other shops. In accordance with its quaint character, Cold Spring only has inns. Guests will be responsible for their own transport to/from wedding events.

The town of Fishkill has several large chain hotels that offer a full suite of guest amenities. The hotels are located about a mile from Fishkill’s Main Street, which has several smaller restaurants and shops. There are also larger restaurants and chain stores in the area. Transportation will be provided between the Fishkill hotels and the wedding.

Things to Do & Eat

Suggested Activities

  • Outdoor aficionados and leaf-peepers (apparently that’s really a thing) come from all over New England to explore the hikes in the region
  • Those looking for a low-key day may like to stroll down Main St. in Cold Spring to do some antiquing, visit one of the many nearby farms to enjoy fall festivals, hot cider and apple picking, or tour the beautiful Boscobel House and Gardens
  • Art lovers should peruse the modern collection at Dia: Beacon or explore the outdoor installations at Storm King
  • Military buffs will want to visit West Point, while culinary buffs will likely be drawn 45 minutes north to the Culinary Institute of America

Getting Around

Garrison, NY is located about 55 miles north of New York City, on the eastern side of the Hudson River. Once you are in the area, all wedding-related locations are within a fifteen minute drive.

Trains

The Metro-North Railroad's Hudson Line provides direct access to the Garrison area from NYC’s Grand Central Terminal. The train is a great option for those wishing to explore The Big Apple before or after the wedding weekend. Train travel times range from 60-75 minutes depending on where you wish to disembark:

  • To get to The Garrison, get off at GARRISON station. The Garrison is about 5 min. from the station, and local taxis will pickup/dropoff there if arrangements are made in advance.
  • To get to Cold Spring, get off at COLD SPRING station. Main St. is about a 10 min walk from the station.
  • To get to Fishkill, get off at BEACON station. Fishkill is about 10-15min. from the station, and taxis should be available at the station or can be arranged in advance.

Planes

NYC’s three major airports, as well as two small regional airports, serve the lower Hudson Valley. If you do fly to a NYC airport, please keep in mind that Friday night traffic around NYC can be heavy and plan accordingly. Approximate driving times for each airport are below:

  • Stewart Airport (SWF): 25 min. to Fishkill, 35 min. to Garrison. Stewart is the closest area airport, but has limited direct flights
  • Westchester County Airport (HPN): 50 min.
  • LaGuardia (LGA): 1 hr. 10 min.
  • Kennedy Airport (JFK): 1 hr. 15 min.
  • Newark Airport (EWR): 1 hr. 20 min.

Automobiles

Garrison is roughly 1 hr. 15 min. from NYC by car.

Once in the area, it is helpful to have access to a car to get around. Several car rental agencies have nearby locations. Please be sure to check operating hours (some are closed on Sundays).

There are also a few taxi services in the area that can be arranged in advance, and will pickup/drop-off at airports and train stations (specific contact info to come).
